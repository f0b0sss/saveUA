Who
we are
Since the first days of the war, the "Save UA" Charity Fund has been actively helping Ukrainian civilians, providing them with food and hygiene products. The organization also supports hospitals, volunteers and citizens who became victims of Russian aggression in Ukraine.
our
goals
We created a fund so that caring people from all over the world could contribute to helping the Ukrainian people. We personally purchase all the necessary resources, deliver them to people, and then send a report to our volunteer angels.

We provide immediate and continuous first aid to vulnerable women, children and displaced persons, so we need support in the procurement of medicines, warm clothes, diapers, food and logistics.

During the period of our work we :

  • We provided about 300 tons of humanitarian aid to the residents of Chernihiv Oblast
  • Organized two charity festivals in Vienna together with KOL Maximiliana
  • We donated $4,500 for a 9-year-old boy who was injured by a rocket
  • More than 400 aid packages were delivered
  • We provided 748 kg of meat for the consumers
  • we provided more than 20 tons of assistance to animals
  • Over 100 children's care packages were provided

Our team

Valentyna

Valentina, a graduate of the Kyiv-Mohyla Academy, majoring in "Finance, Banking and Insurance". Before the start of the full-scale invasion, she devoted all her time to studying, self-development and organizing student events, as well as engaged in projects in the field of logistics and cryptocurrency. She decided to volunteer because she simply could not stay away from all the horrors that happened to her native country. Valentina is sure that we will win, because the truth is on our side, but for this the Ukrainian people must stick together and help each other.

Julia

Julia, an artist and a model from Chernihiv. According to Julia, she was the only person in Chernihiv who since the first days of the invasion talked to foreigners in English, trying to draw their attention to the situation in Chernihiv region. Oficial media didn’t speak sufficiently about what horrors all the people from that area experienced during Russian occupation and shelling, especially in foreign languages. That’s why she decided to take advantage of her publicity and ask foreign followers on social media for help. Hopefully, there were some activists who responded to her request and sent some humanitarian aid, and still do it.

Karyna

Karyna, social media manager. She creates content for our fund’s social media and makes it visible online. She joined the fund because she wanted both to feel that she was helping her country and people and to practice her social media management skills she obtained at an online course.

Daryna

Daryna, has been working in the FinTech field for more than 5 years, now at the Binance exchange. Together with her colleagues she created a charity fund to help people, affected by the war. Daryna was also engaged into charity activities before the war and regularly helped elderly people.

Vladyslava

Vladyslava, works for an IT company that develops blockchain projects. She has always had an idea to create something that would help society, so a couple of months before the start of the war, she with her partners created a fund for landscaping Ukraine and planned to plant trees. However, the war forced them to change priorities, so they reformatted the project. They started buying food, which the drivers took to more dangerous areas where people had no medicines, clothing, food, hygiene items, etc. Currently Vladyslava is responsible for coordinating aid and fundraising at the fund.

Valeriya

Valeriya, a graduate of the Kyiv University of Economics majoring in "International Economics", currently a student of the Vienna University of Economics. Although Valeriya was in Austria at the beginning of the invasion and had no threat to her life, she still could not leave everything aside, because all her relatives and friends were in Ukraine. Ukraine is a part of her heart, her home. The girl felt uncontrollable pain and fear for her country while listening to the stories and watching the news. However, as you all know, there is no way to overcome grief with tears and sorrow, so she decided to get down to business and started volunteering with Caritas at the main train station in Vienna: she distributed food to people who left Ukraine, explained the route, helped to find shelter. Later, she came up with the idea of organizing the first Ukrainian Food Market in Vienna, where she met other members of the fund.
